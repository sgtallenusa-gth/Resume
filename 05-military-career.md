# Military Career

## United States Army, Army National Guard, and Army Reserve

Service began in 1998 at age 17. Military experience includes combat arms, logistics, transportation, unit movement, civil response, resilience training, instructor development, and battalion operations.

## Key Timeline

- 1998: Joined the U.S. Army and initially trained as 19D Cavalry Scout.
- Later reclassified to 11C Infantry Mortarman.
- 2000: Completed PLDC.
- Cross-trained as 77F, then dual-trained in 92A and 92Y to fill unit vacancies.
- 2004 to 2005: Deployed to Iraq.
- 2007: Completed Army Master Driver Course and served as Battalion Master Driver across assignments until 2023.
- 2018: Trained as 13B Cannon Crewmember.
- 2019: Completed ALC as 13B30.
- 2020: Promoted to E6 during COVID.
- 2020 to 2022: Served on full-time orders supporting civil response missions including riot, hurricane, and blizzard response.
- 2021: Completed UMO course and served as Battalion UMO during JRTC mobilization and subsequent overseas deployment preparation.
- 2020 to 2021: Became Assistant Battalion S4 NCOIC and covered S4 NCOIC duties for approximately 14 months while also leading a Howitzer Section.
- 2022: Completed MRT course and served as MRT and Suicide Intervention Officer.
- 2023: Completed CFD-IC and became an Army instructor, later cross-trained as 88M30.

## Leadership Lessons

Military service shaped my approach to technology leadership through Composite Risk Management, MDMP, mission planning, logistics discipline, documentation, training, and leader development.

## The Gerber

A Battalion XO nicknamed me "The Gerber" because I became the go-to tool for every mission. That nickname reflects a consistent career pattern: learn what the mission requires, fill the gap, solve the problem, and teach others to carry it forward.
