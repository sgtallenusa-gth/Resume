# Case Study: ADP Enterprise Migration Recovery

## Context

At ADP, I initially joined as a contract employee to assist with a migration from an on-premises Oracle Forms and Reports implementation to a multi-site, load-balanced Oracle WebLogic deployment.

My assigned role during cutover was small: assist with turning off the legacy on-premises services and hand off to the project lead.

## Challenge

At approximately 3:00 a.m. during the cutover, executive leadership called me onto the conference bridge because the new infrastructure was failing. The project lead had strong Microsoft experience but limited Linux experience, and significant mistakes had been made in the new environment.

Rollback was no longer an option. The on-premises systems had already been powered down and hard drives had been removed.

## Action

Over the next 48 hours, I assessed the failed deployment, rebuilt critical portions of the Linux and Oracle WebLogic platform, recovered core datasets, and stabilized the environment enough to resume production operations.

After the recovery, I was hired full time to manage and maintain the platform.

## Modernization

Once the environment stabilized, I built and leveraged Ansible, Puppet, and Jenkins systems to automate repetitive tasks and reduce human error.

I entered the project with limited Java, Oracle Forms, and Oracle Reports experience, but rapidly taught myself the required platform knowledge and used Oracle enterprise support to accelerate learning.

## Results

- Recovered a failed enterprise cutover when rollback was no longer possible.
- Restored mission-critical platform functionality under severe time pressure.
- Earned a full-time engineering role based on crisis performance.
- Introduced automation to improve consistency and reduce operational risk.
