# Case Study: Army STARRS Site Management

## Role

Site Manager, Fort Jackson, South Carolina

## Dates

2010 to 2013

## Context

I served as Site Manager for the Army STARRS project conducted by the University of Michigan at Fort Jackson.

The project gathered structured survey data from Initial Entry Soldiers and followed participants through their careers to help establish baselines for PTSD, suicide risk, resilience, and behavioral health research.

## Responsibilities

I was responsible for the technology environment supporting the research mission, including:

- Servers.
- Network infrastructure.
- 400+ computers used for structured survey collection.
- Local technical operations.
- System availability during scheduled research events.
- Coordination with research and military stakeholders.

## Mission Impact

The role required reliable infrastructure, disciplined operations, data integrity, repeatability, and strong coordination between civilian researchers and military organizations.

## Career Impact

This assignment strengthened my approach to mission-critical IT operations, documentation, repeatable process design, and supporting sensitive government-aligned research environments.
