# Case Study: Unitrends Disaster Recovery and Sales Engineering

## Role Progression

Level I Engineer -> Level II / Mentor -> Sales Engineer

## Dates

2013 to 2015

## Context

Unitrends was a backup and disaster recovery company in Columbia, South Carolina. I started in frontline support, advanced into escalation and mentorship, then transitioned into Sales Engineering.

## Technical Support

I supported backup and disaster recovery environments across customer infrastructure. The work involved troubleshooting complex issues, understanding customer environments, and helping restore confidence during high-stress incidents.

## Mentorship

As a Level II engineer and mentor, I helped junior engineers build technical skills, improve troubleshooting discipline, and communicate more effectively with customers.

## Sales Engineering

I transitioned into Sales Engineering, where I learned to translate technical capabilities into business outcomes. This included customer discovery, solution alignment, technical explanation, proof-of-concept support, and helping customers understand the operational value of disaster recovery architecture.

## Career Impact

Unitrends became the bridge between technical support and solution architecture. It strengthened my customer-facing communication, business continuity knowledge, and ability to explain complex systems to technical and non-technical stakeholders.
