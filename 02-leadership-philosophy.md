# Leadership Philosophy

Leadership is not about being the smartest person in the room. It is about learning people, recognizing their strengths, and building the team to fit the mission.

That lesson came from a Battalion Commander and became one of the strongest influences on how I lead technical teams.

## Leadership Creed

> You can't teach what you don't know, and you can't lead where you won't go.

Credibility is earned through action. If I expect a team to learn, document, test, improve, and take ownership, I have to model those behaviors myself.

## Team Building

Great teams are built around complementary strengths, mutual trust, shared ownership, and a clear mission.

My job as a leader is to identify talent, remove obstacles, create opportunities for growth, and make sure each person understands how their contribution supports the mission.

## Mentorship Model

Teach One -> Show One -> Do One

As people gain confidence, the model reverses:

Do One -> Show One -> Teach One

The goal is not to build systems that depend on me. The goal is to build teams and organizations that continue to succeed without me as a single point of failure.
